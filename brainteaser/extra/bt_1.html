Question 1

If you look at a clock and the time is 12:15, what is the angle between the hour and the minute hand?

Correct

Incorrect

It is not 90 degrees! The hour hand is always moving, it moves 30 degrees every hour. Therefore it will move 30/4 = 7.5 degrees in 15 minutes. The angle between the hands is thus 90 - 7.5 = 82.5 degrees.

Question 2

A car is on a journey that is 120km long. It travels the first 60km at an average speed of 30km per hour. How fast would the car have to travel in order have an average speed of 60km per hour over the whole journey?

It is a trick question, it is not possible. An infinite speed is required. Since the car has already travelled 60 km at 30 km per hour, there is only 60km left which is not enough distance to get the average speed up to 60km per hour.

Question 5

You have 25 horse and you want to know the 3 fastest. You can race 5 horses at a time. You can only record the order the horses finish in each race, you cannot time them. What is the least number of races you have to hold to find the fastest 3?

Correct

Incorrect

The answer is 7. First race all of the horses in 5 races. Record the horses that come in first, second and third in each race. Then race all the horses together in race number 6. The winner of this race is the fastest. Take the horses who came second and third place from the winners first race, the horse that came second in race number 6 and the horse that came second in its race and the horse that came third in race 6 and race them to find second and third.

Question 6

You have a five-litre jug and a three-litre jug. You must obtain exactly four litres of water. How will you do it?

Fill the five-litre jug and then pour it into the three-litre jug. The five-litre jug has 2 litres left. Now throw the three-litre jug of water away. Pour the 2 litres from the five-litre jug to the three-litre jug. So now the three-litre jug has just 2 litres of water. Fill the five-litre jug again, and pour 1 litre to the three-litre jug. You are then left with 4 litres of water in the five-litre jug.

Question 7

You play a game with a friend where you flip a coin. If you get heads you win, if not then it is your friends turn. Is it better to go first or second in this game?

It is better to go first. The first player has a 50/50 chance of winning where as the second player needs the first person to get tails (0.5 chance) and then must get heads (0.5 chance) which has a probability of 0.25.

Question 8

What is the sum of all of the integers between 1 and 100, there is a quick trick to solve this quickly.

Correct

Incorrect

Add: 1 + 100 = 101, 2 + 99 = 101, 3 + 98 = 101,...., 50+51 = 101. This gives 50 x 101 = 5050.

Question 9

You have 4 blue socks, 4 red socks and 2 green socks in a drawer. It is dark while you are packing your bag for holidays and you cannot see what colour the socks are. What is the least number of socks you need to take to ensure you have at least 1 pair of matching socks?

Correct

Incorrect

You have 3 possible colours. Therefore you need to pack 4 socks to ensure 2 of them are the same colour.

Question 10

You are given 9 weights that look the same, but 1 of them weighs slightly less than the other 8. You are also given a balance scale. What is the LEAST number of times you could use the balance to determine which of the weights is the lighter one?

Correct

Incorrect

On the first weighing, you would weigh three weights on each side. Either one side of the scales will be lighter or else they are the same weight, thus the group with the lighter weight can be identified. Then you would place one weight on each side of scale, and leave one off. This method will identify the light weight while only performing two weightings in total.
